## How to log in (first time)

1. Check your email (`clara.jean.418@gmail.com`) for a message from Netlify titled something like *"You've been invited to Clara's Critters"*. If you don't see it, check your spam folder.
2. Click the **Accept the invite** button in that email.
3. You'll land on your website. A little box will pop up asking you to set a password — pick something you'll remember.
4. Once you've set a password, you'll be sent into the editor automatically.

After the first time, you just go to **clarascritters.com/admin** and sign in with your email and that password.

## What the editor looks like

When you log in, you'll see a list down the left side. These are all the parts of your site you can edit:

- **Site settings** — your business name, tagline, phone number, email, the big photo at the top of the page.
- **About Clara** — your bio and your portrait photo.
- **Services** — the list of things you offer.
- **Rates** — your prices.
- **Gallery** — photos of pets you've cared for.
- **Testimonials** — quotes from happy clients.

Click any of those, make your changes, and hit **Publish** in the top right. About a minute later, your changes are live.

## Common things you'll want to do

### Change your rates

1. Click **Rates** in the left sidebar.
2. Find the row you want to change. Update the price (or anything else).
3. Click **Publish** in the top right.

To **add** a new rate:
1. Click **Rates** → **Add Rates** at the bottom of the list.
2. Fill in service, length, price, and a note if you want.
3. Publish.

To **remove** a rate, click the trash can icon next to it.

### Update your bio

1. Click **About Clara** in the sidebar.
2. Edit the **Bio** text box. Press Enter twice between paragraphs to make a new paragraph.
3. Publish.

### Replace your portrait photo

1. Click **About Clara**.
2. Click on the current portrait photo.
3. Choose **Upload new** and pick a photo from your computer.
4. Type a short description in **Portrait alt text** (this helps people who use screen readers — something like "Clara smiling on a hike with her dog").
5. Publish.

### Add a photo to the gallery

1. Click **Gallery** in the sidebar.
2. Click **Add Photos** at the bottom of the list.
3. Click the image upload area, pick a photo from your computer.
4. Type a short **alt text** (description of the photo).
5. Optionally write a **caption** that visitors will see under the photo.
6. Publish.

### Replace a placeholder testimonial with a real review

1. Click **Testimonials** in the sidebar.
2. Click the placeholder you want to replace.
3. Update the **Quote**, **Author**, and **Pet name**.
4. Publish.

### Change your tagline or hero photo

1. Click **Site settings** in the sidebar.
2. Update the **Tagline** or click on the **Hero photo** to upload a new one.
3. Publish.

### Change the email shown on the site

> Note: this is just the email **shown to visitors**. Where contact form submissions actually go is set up in Netlify and changes to that need Garrett.

1. Click **Site settings**.
2. Update **Public contact email**.
3. Publish.

## How visitors contact you

When someone fills out the contact form on your site, two things happen:

1. **You get an email** at `clara.jean.418@gmail.com` with everything they wrote.
2. **A copy is saved** in your Netlify dashboard as a backup, in case the email goes missing.

You don't need to log in to Netlify for the normal flow — just check your email.

> The first time someone submits the form, the notification email might land in spam. Mark it as "not spam" once and Gmail will learn.

## A few small rules

- **Always click Publish.** Saving a draft doesn't put your changes on the live site. The big button in the top right is the one that matters.
- **Photos:** anything reasonable from your phone or camera works. The site will automatically resize them to load fast.
- **Don't worry about breaking the layout.** The editor only lets you change words and pictures, not the design. You literally cannot break anything from this admin screen.
- **Wait about a minute** after publishing for changes to appear on the live site. If they don't show up, refresh the page.

## When something feels wrong

- **You can't log in.** Try resetting your password from the login screen. If that doesn't work, text Garrett.
- **You published something and now it looks weird.** No problem — go back into the editor, fix it, and publish again. Every change is reversible.
- **The contact form notifications stopped arriving.** Check your spam folder first. If they're really gone, text Garrett — it's probably a Netlify setting.
- **You want to change something the editor doesn't let you change** (colors, layout, the order of sections). Text Garrett.
